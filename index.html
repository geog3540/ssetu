Family Household Ration at Block Group Level, IA

This map represents family household density at block groups level in the State of Iowa. The purpose of this mapping project is to identify areas with highest and lowest density and the underlying factors.The data for this mapping project comes from NHGIS. The data includes block groups for the state of Iowa, and household numbers for the years 2019 to 2023, sourced from NHGIS

Statewide School Enrollment in 2023, IA

The map depicts the total number of school enrollment (in millions) up to 10th grades across the States in 2023. The dots represent enrollment totals for individual states showing significant variation in educational participation. Enrollment ranges widely, from 3.9 millions to 38 millions, suggesting disparities in population size or education access across regions. The color gradient represents enrollment intensity, with light to darker hues corresponding to low to higher numbers of enrollment. Data includes State boundaries shapefiles, and school enrollment numbers in each state for the years 2023,sourced from NHGIS.

Median Household Income vs % Affordable Housing Units per Block Groups in Johnson County, IA

Affordable housing is a pressing urban problem that cities are dealing with. Like many cities in the United States, Iowa City has affordable housing problem created by the high demand and inadequate supply in housing.Johnson County has the second most expensive housing market in the state of Iowa (City, Affordable Housing Action Plan, 2022). This map visualizes spatial association between low affordability of rental houses and low income communities in Johnson County, IA. Data were collected from NHGIS for the years 2019-2023. The number of affordable units were calculated based on the number of rental units as a function of household income (filtered the units equal or below 30% household income)

Bikeshare Flows within Chicago, IL from April 2021

The map represents the bikeshare in Chicago during April 2021, which is the movement of bikes in a bikeshare system, how they move between stations by the users. This is a helpful analysis that can help the city to identify needs, demand patterns and commuting trends.

Critical Facilities in Iowa at 500-year Return Period Flood Risk

At the onset of flooding or any other natural disasters, the critical facilities such as hospital, police, fire services etc. play a significant role in minimizing the damage and effects. However, the effects of flooding on critical facilities are also significantly increasing.This map shows ciritcal facilities established in 500-years return period floodplain in the State of Iowa.
